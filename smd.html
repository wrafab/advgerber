Surface Mount Devices (SMD)

Surface Mount Devices

Surface mount devices are mounted directly to the printed circuit board as opposed to through hole soldering. Surface mount printed circuit boards are easier to create, but harder to solder. They use liquid flux and contray to through hole, use solder directly from the tip of the soldering iron. SMD's come in different sizing and identifying polarity can be confusing. The best way to learn SMD soldering is to practice. You may need to use the microscope to place the components on the board.
